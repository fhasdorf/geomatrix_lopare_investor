# The Lopare Opportunity

> *Draft text — pending ARCore approval. Last updated 2026-06-20.*

## 1. The Thesis

The Lopare project hosts a defined sediment-hosted lithium and boron Mineral
Resource of **1,286 Mt at 574 ppm Li₂O and 0.75% B₂O₃** (CSA Global, 2022),
positioned within an 87.7 km² licensed exploration area in Bosnia and
Herzegovina. The project is at an advanced exploration stage with 71 drill
holes completed across three campaigns (2020–2022) and a completed
NI 43-101-equivalent resource estimate. Lopare offers a rare combination
within the European battery materials supply chain: a multi-commodity asset
in an EU candidate jurisdiction with an existing resource base.

## 2. Why Now

The European Critical Raw Materials Act (CRMA), in force since 2024,
establishes binding domestic production targets for strategic raw materials.
Lithium and boron both feature on the CRMA strategic materials list.
Existing European lithium production capacity is limited and concentrated in
a handful of project pipelines, most of which face long permitting timelines.

Boron supply is similarly concentrated, with the majority of global production
controlled by two companies. Specialty borates are a critical input for solar
glass, batteries, and high-end ceramics — all sectors with double-digit
growth trajectories.

Lopare's geological setting, jurisdiction, and stage of exploration position
the project to participate in this market shift within the next investment
cycle.

## 3. The Lopare Edge

**Jurisdiction.** Bosnia and Herzegovina is an EU candidate country with a
mining-friendly regulatory framework and existing infrastructure (rail, road,
grid power, water) within reach of the license area.

**Multi-commodity.** The resource hosts both lithium and boron in economically
significant grades, providing natural revenue diversification and shared
infrastructure costs through the development phase.

**Advanced exploration stage.** With 71 drill holes, a defined Mineral
Resource Estimate, and metallurgical test work in progress, Lopare is past
the highest-uncertainty phase of exploration and can proceed directly to
Pre-Feasibility Study work.

**Sediment-hosted geology.** Unlike pegmatite hard-rock lithium deposits,
Lopare's lithium is hosted in clay and tuffaceous sediments. This geology
type — exemplified by projects like Thacker Pass (Nevada) and Rhyolite Ridge
(Nevada) — typically supports lower-CAPEX processing and conventional mining
methods.
